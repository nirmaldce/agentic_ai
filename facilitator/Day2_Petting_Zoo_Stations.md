# 🎪 AI Petting Zoo — Station Cards

> Print one A4 per station. Place at each table. Groups of 3–4 rotate every **7 minutes** when the bell rings.
> Rule of the room: **No instruction. Just play.** If you're stuck, ask the next table.

---

## 🟦 STATION 1 — NotebookLM

**What:** Upload your PDFs. Get a podcast, study guide, mind map.
**Why faculty care:** Turn your lecture notes into audio lectures in 60 seconds.
**URL:** https://notebooklm.google.com

**Try this:**
1. Upload any PDF from your laptop (syllabus / paper / textbook chapter)
2. Click **"Audio Overview"** → wait 60 sec → play the podcast
3. Ask in chat: *"Generate 10 MCQs from this document with answer key"*

**Wow moment:** Two AI hosts having a real conversation about *your* PDF.

---

## 🟦 STATION 2 — ChatGPT vs Gemini vs Claude

**What:** Same prompt, three brains. Spot the difference.
**Why faculty care:** Know which tool to reach for, when.
**URLs:** chat.openai.com • gemini.google.com • claude.ai

**Try this — paste the SAME prompt into all three:**
> *"I teach Data Structures to 2nd-year CSE. Give me 5 real-world analogies for explaining recursion to students who've never coded. Be vivid."*

**Compare:**
- Which answer is clearest?
- Which is most creative?
- Which would you actually use Monday?

**Bonus:** Ask each one *"What is today's date?"* — see who admits they don't know.

---

## 🟦 STATION 3 — Perplexity

**What:** Google + ChatGPT had a baby. Answers with **citations**.
**Why faculty care:** Research without the hallucinations.
**URL:** https://perplexity.ai

**Try this:**
1. Ask: *"What are the latest 2025 advances in [your research area]?"*
2. Click the citation numbers — see the actual sources
3. Then ask: *"Summarise the most cited paper in a single paragraph for an undergrad"*

**Wow moment:** Real URLs you can verify. No more "trust me bro" from AI.

---

## 🟦 STATION 4 — Gamma / Napkin AI

**What:** Type text → get slides or diagrams. Instantly.
**Why faculty care:** 1-hour PPT becomes 5 minutes.
**URLs:** https://gamma.app • https://napkin.ai

**Try this on Gamma:**
1. Click **"Generate"**
2. Type: *"A 10-slide lecture on Newton's Laws of Motion for first-year engineering"*
3. Watch slides appear with images and layout

**Try this on Napkin:**
1. Paste 2 paragraphs from any topic you teach
2. Click any sentence → "Generate visual"
3. Pick a diagram style

**Wow moment:** Your lecture notes → publishable visuals in 30 seconds.

---

## 🟦 STATION 5 — Suno (Music)

**What:** Type lyrics → get a real song.
**Why faculty care:** Mnemonic songs. Course jingles. Department anthem 😄
**URL:** https://suno.com

**Try this:**
1. Click **"Create"**
2. Paste lyrics like:
> *"OSI seven layers, from physical to app, / Data link and network, transport is the map…"*
3. Pick a style: "upbeat pop" / "classical Indian"
4. Wait 30 sec → press play

**Wow moment:** Students will *actually* remember the OSI layers now.

---

## 🟦 STATION 6 — Ideogram (Images)

**What:** Type → get an image. Better at text-in-image than most.
**Why faculty care:** Custom diagrams, slide visuals, cover images. Stop stealing from Google.
**URL:** https://ideogram.ai

**Try this:**
1. Prompt: *"A clean infographic explaining photosynthesis, labelled in English, flat illustration style, light background"*
2. Try again: *"A vintage textbook diagram of a transistor with hand-drawn labels"*
3. Download the one you like

**Wow moment:** Royalty-free, on-brand, made-to-order visuals.

---

## 🟦 STATION 7 — HuggingChat / LM Studio

**What:** Free, open models with a UI. Like ChatGPT but no login, no cost.
**Why faculty care:** Privacy + zero cost + many model choice.
**URL:** https://huggingface.co/chat (or LM Studio if installed locally)

**Try this:**
1. Top-right: switch the model (try Llama 3.3, Mistral, Qwen)
2. Same prompt across 3 models: *"Explain the Halting Problem to a 12-year-old"*
3. Notice which model "feels" different

**Wow moment:** No subscription. No data lock-in. You're in control.

---

## 🟦 STATION 8 — GitHub Copilot (Live Demo)

**What:** AI writes code as you type.
**Why faculty care:** Even non-programmers can build small tools.
**URL:** Open VS Code (already installed on facilitator laptop)

**Watch the facilitator demo OR try yourself if Copilot is logged in:**
1. Open a blank `.py` file
2. Type a *comment*: `# function to read a CSV of student marks and print toppers`
3. Press Tab → watch code appear
4. Run it on a sample CSV

**Wow moment:** You described the problem in English. The computer wrote the code.
